What makes a light bulb light up?

The typical incandescent light bulb contains a thin wire (usually tungsten) called a filament that has a high electrical resistance. This filament gets very hot when an electric current passes through it. The intense temperature makes the filament glow brightly.

The Ohm's Law Equation

Ohm’s law states the relationship between electric current and potential difference. The current that flows through most conductors is directly proportional to the voltage applied to it. Georg Simon Ohm, a German physicist was the first to verify Ohm’s law experimentally. Ohm expressed his discovery in the form of a simple equation, describing how voltage, current, and resistance interrelate: Voltage(E) = Current(I)Resistance(R)
